 Exploratry and predective data analysis for SFCAPC

Developed as a part of DataKind hackathon with a team of data scientists from different walks of life.

Problem:

Objective of this project was to predict clients who will succeed at building protective factors, so that SFACPC could better allocate staff, time and resources.

Process:

As part of the DataKind hackathon I got to work for San Francisco Child Abuse Prevention Center which provides targeted intervention program to high risk families in order to build protective fators and prevent child abuse.

  • We started with raw data that the center had collected over two years on the families that go through its programs and their scores at various points in the program
  • To understand the data better we built out a data model. This helped us in outlining the major predective factors that could affect the outcome, ie, succeeding the program. At this point we hypothesised that demographics and the interventions that the family recieves would have an affect on building protective factors in individual.
  • Before we could explore the data any further we had to clean the data and turn it into a form that could be used in further analysis.
  • We found that the zip code of families ,ie, their neighbourhood has an affect on their likelyhood to succeed in the progam.
  • We conducted least square regression on the data and found out that some of the interventions like adult counselling and care anagement has higher impact on building protective factors. In general, we also found that the more time an individual spends in the interventions, more likely theya re to succeed.

Technologies and skills used:

Statistical analysis using R
