Student Budgeting Tool

Developed as a part of semester long project for User Interface Design and Development class in a team of five with Andrew Wu, Molly Mahar, Pooja Jain and Astika Gupta.

Problem:

The Problem statement driving this project was "How to help Berkeley students finance better?"

Process:

We started out the process by outlining the major questions we wanted answers to, like do students budget at all? If they do, what tools do they use? Are their any difficulties they face with these tools? Where are the information gaps? Do students of different age group and nationality think differently about money? To get answers to these questions we conducted several interviews. We focused on covering as many distinct groups of students as possible and tried to understand their frustrations around money matters. Based on the interviews, we designed four personas based on the level of planning students do to manage their finances.

Next in the design process we made several lo-fi prototype keeping in mind the goals of our primary persona. We tested different versions of the lo-fi prototype and collected feedback from the target users. BAsed on these feedback we designed a few versions of interactive prototypes adn tested it more. By this point, we were able to flesh out the components we wanted to keep in our final version of the tool based on all the feedback we had recieved. These would solve the problems of our primary personas without completely ignoring the secondary ones.

Based on the work done on all the previosuse prototype we developed a hi-fi prototype of the tool. We conducted qualitative and quantitative usability tests on our hi-fi prototype to fully understand whats working and whats not in order to improve the tool in future versions.

Skills used:

  • Contextual inquiry and interviews
  • Persona creation
  • Paper prototyping
  • Interaction design
  • Usability testing
