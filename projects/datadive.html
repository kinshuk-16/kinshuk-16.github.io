Exploratry and predective data analysis for social good

Developed as a part of DataKind hackathon with a team of data scientists. Organization: SFCAPC (San Fransisco Child Abuse Protection Center)

Problem:

Objective of this project was to predict clients who will succeed at building protective factors, so that SFACPC could better allocate staff, time and resources.

Process:

As part of the DataKind hackathon I got to work for San Francisco Child Abuse Prevention Center which provides targeted intervention program to high risk families in order to build protective fators and prevent child abuse.

We started with raw data that the center had collected over two years on the families that go through its programs and their scores at various points in the program. To understand the data better we built out a data model. This helped us in outlining the major predective factors that could affect the outcome, ie, succeeding the program. At this point we hypothesised that demographics and the interventions that the family recieves would have an affect on building protective factors in individual. We then Preprocessed all our data to make it in a form amenable to further analysis.

We conducted a lot of exploratory and predective analysis on the data. Using least square regression (OLS) on the data we found out that some of the interventions like adult counselling and care anagement has higher impact on building protective factors. In general, we also found that the more time an individual spends in the interventions, more likely they are to succeed. Another key finding was that the zip code of families ,ie, their neighbourhood has an affect on their likelyhood to succeed in the progam.

Skills used:

  • Statistical analysis using R
  • Data modelling
